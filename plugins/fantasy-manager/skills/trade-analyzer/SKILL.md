---
name: trade-analyzer
description: Judge whether a proposed fantasy trade is fair and good for your roster, and draft a counter if it is not. Works for any fantasy sport and any connected data provider.
---

When asked to evaluate a trade:

1. List what each side gives and gets. Value each player on rest-of-season outlook, not the last box
   score. If a fantasy data provider MCP is connected (Sleeper, ESPN, Yahoo, etc.), use it to pull
   usage and matchup/schedule data to ground each valuation; otherwise reason from what the manager
   tells you.
2. Score raw value: is the talent/production roughly even, or is one side clearly winning?
3. Score roster FIT, which can override raw value:
   - Are you trading from a position of surplus into a position of need? That is a win even at a
     slight value loss.
   - Does the trade leave a starting slot, bye/off-day, or scoring category thin? That is a hidden
     cost.
   - Account for league scoring: points vs. category leagues change everything. In category leagues
     (common in basketball and baseball), weigh which categories each side helps or hurts; in points
     leagues, weigh total output and scoring quirks (PPR, superflex, etc.).
4. Give a verdict: Accept / Decline / Counter, in one line.
5. If Counter, draft the specific counteroffer and the one-sentence pitch to send the other manager.

A trade that loses a little raw value but fixes your starting lineup or a weak category is usually a
win. Say so plainly. The provider only supplies the inputs; the judgment is sport- and
provider-independent.
