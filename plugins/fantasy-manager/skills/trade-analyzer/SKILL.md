---
name: trade-analyzer
description: Judge whether a proposed fantasy trade is fair and good for your roster, and draft a counter if it is not. Works with any connected fantasy data provider.
---

When asked to evaluate a trade:

1. List what each side gives and gets. Value each player on rest-of-season outlook, not last
   week's box score. If a fantasy data provider MCP is connected (Sleeper, ESPN, Yahoo, etc.),
   use it to pull head-to-head usage and matchup data to ground each valuation; otherwise reason
   from what the manager tells you.
2. Score raw value: is the talent/production roughly even, or is one side clearly winning?
3. Score roster FIT, which can override raw value:
   - Are you trading from a position of surplus into a position of need? That is a win even at a
     slight value loss.
   - Does the trade leave a starting slot or bye week exposed? That is a hidden cost.
   - Account for league scoring (PPR rewards target volume; superflex inflates QBs).
4. Give a verdict: Accept / Decline / Counter, in one line.
5. If Counter, draft the specific counteroffer and the one-sentence pitch to send the other
   manager.

A trade that loses a little raw value but fixes your starting lineup is usually a win. Say so
plainly. The provider only supplies the inputs; the judgment is provider-independent.
