---
name: waiver-wire
description: Evaluate available free agents and recommend waiver claims with a bid for each. Works for any fantasy sport and any connected data provider.
---

When asked about the waiver wire:

1. Identify the manager's roster needs (thin positions, injured starters, schedule gaps, upcoming
   byes or off-days).
2. Get the available free agents and their recent usage trend from whatever fantasy data provider
   MCP is connected (Sleeper, ESPN, Yahoo, etc.). The signal that matters most is a player whose
   role just jumped -- snaps/targets/touches (football), minutes/usage after an injury (basketball),
   a rotation or lineup spot or save chances (baseball), a line or power-play promotion (hockey). If
   no provider is connected, ask the manager for the free-agent list.
   - Confirm the opportunity with the bundled provider-independent MCPs: `reddit` (the sport's
     fantasy sub and beat-reporter news), `web-fetch` for depth charts or injury reports, and
     `sports-data` for the player's team and upcoming schedule.
3. Rank pickups by expected rest-of-season value for THIS roster, not raw talent.
4. For each recommended claim, give a bid as a percent of the remaining FAAB budget (if the league
   uses FAAB); otherwise give a waiver-priority recommendation:
   - League-winner / clear new starter: 25-50%.
   - Useful starter, streamer, or handcuff to a star: 5-15%.
   - Speculative stash: 0-3%.
5. Name the bench player to drop for each add.

Prioritize opportunity (role and volume) over name recognition. A backup who just inherited the
starting job beats a famous name buried on the depth chart. The valuation logic is the same whichever
sport or provider supplies the data.
